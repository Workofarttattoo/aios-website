Data Requirements & Preparation

What Data Does the Tool Need?

Quantum prediction tools require historical price/metric data to identify patterns. Minimum requirements:

  • Minimum 60 data points (60 days for daily data, 60 weeks for weekly, etc.)
  • Preferred 300+ data points for optimal accuracy (1+ year of daily data)
  • No gaps in data (weekends/holidays are automatically handled)
  • Clean data without extreme outliers or delisted periods

Step 5: Generate prediction with confidence bounds

Confidence Score Interpretation

Confidence Range Signal Strength Interpretation
80-100% Very Strong High certainty. Strong directional bias detected. Consider position size increase.
65-80% Strong Good confidence. Proceed with normal position sizes. Monitor for reversals.
50-65% Moderate Fair confidence. Use as confirmation tool only. Combine with other analysis.
<50% Weak Low confidence. Mixed signals. Skip prediction or use extremely small position.

Risk Metrics Explained

Volatility = Standard Deviation of Returns
Higher volatility = more unpredictable price swings
(Stocks: 10-20% typical | Crypto: 30-80% typical)
Sharpe Ratio = (Expected Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Risk
Values: >1.0 is good, >2.0 is excellent
Indicates reward per unit of risk taken
Maximum Drawdown = Peak-to-trough decline
Example: -15% max drawdown = worst loss from peak in period
Risk tolerance guide: Conservative <10%, Moderate <20%, Aggressive <40%

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why was a prediction wrong?

A: Quantum models are statistical, not fortune tellers. They identify patterns from 70+ years of market data, achieving 65-75% accuracy. "Wrong" predictions are part of normal operation. Always use stop-losses.

Q: Which tool should I use?

A: Match tool to your interest: stocks (Bear Tame), crypto (CryptoVault), weather (WeatherQuantum), legal cases (LegalPredictor), patents (PatentDiscovery).

Q: How often should I check predictions?

A: For active trading: daily or weekly. For longer timeframes (3+ months): weekly or monthly. New data = new insights. Re-run predictions every 5-7 days.

Q: Can I use predictions for different timeframes?

A: Yes! Get both 7-day AND 30-day predictions. Signals should align for strongest conviction. Conflicting signals = caution.

Q: What about market shocks (black swans)?

A: Models can't predict unprecedented events (wars, pandemics, regulatory changes). If risks change, predictions become invalid. Always maintain stop-losses.

Q: How much money should I risk per prediction?

A: Position sizing rule: Risk no more than 1-2% of account per trade. If AAPL prediction has 2.2% downside risk and you have $50K account, risk max $1K (roughly 6-7 shares at $150).
